Lesson One: General Introduction to concepts of nutrition and photosynthesis 

• Nutrition is the series of process by which living organisms obtain food substances and use them to provide energy and materials for their growth, activities and reproduction.

• It is therefore a basic characteristic of life.

• Food is a complex energy-rich organic matter, which living organism feed on to obtain substances necessary for life. Plants are the earth’s most important food producers; their type of feeding is called autotrophic nutrition.

• It always involves the synthesis of organic compounds from inorganic raw materials and it is sometimes called holophytic nutrition.

• Plant manufacture their own food through the process of photosynthesis. Photosynthesis is the chemical process through which plant some bacteria and algae produce glucose and oxygen from carbon dioxide and water using only light as source of energy. 

• In plant leaf, excess glucose is rapidly converted to starch. Therefore leaves can be tested to show that photosynthesis has occur rather than testing for glucose.

• Light and chlorophyll are required for this process, this is usually indicated by adding them to the equation near the arrow.
